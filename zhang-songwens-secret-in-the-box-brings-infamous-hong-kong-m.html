Zhang Songwen’s ‘Secret in the Box’ Brings Infamous Hong Kong Murder Case to Shanghai Competition

United States · 2026-06-21

Zhang Songwen, one of China’s most revered actors, is back in the spotlight with a chilling new film that has captivated audiences at the Shanghai International Film Festival. Titled *Secret in the Box*, the movie dives into one of Hong Kong’s most notorious unsolved sagas: the 1974 cardboard box murder. The case has haunted the city for decades, and the convicted man always claimed innocence until his death. Now, Zhang Songwen’s portrayal brings this dark chapter to mainland screens, sparking fresh debate about justice, guilt, and the power of cinema to resurrect buried truths.

The Infamous Hong Kong Case Reimagined

The 1974 cardboard box murder case is a legend in Hong Kong’s criminal history. A woman’s body was discovered stuffed inside a wooden box, and the subsequent trial led to a death sentence for a man who repeatedly swore he was framed. For years, true crime enthusiasts and legal experts have argued over whether the real perpetrator evaded capture. By adapting this story, *Secret in the Box* doesn’t just retell events—it probes the psychological trenches of the accused, with Zhang Songwen delivering a performance that blurs the line between victim and villain. The Shanghai competition serves as a fitting arena for this gritty narrative, known for showcasing films that challenge societal norms and judicial fallacies.

A Glimpse into the Festival’s Daring Lineup

This year’s Shanghai festival is unflinching in its choices. Alongside Zhang Songwen’s film, another entry features a clerk who boils a shark alive, a surreal yet allegorical tale about moral decay in modern workplaces. These selections highlight a trend in Chinese cinema: embracing uncomfortable, violent histories to question established truths. The cardboard box murder film, in particular, resonates because it asks if the system can ever correct a mistake. Zhang Songwen’s role as the beleaguered suspect is being hailed as career-defining, especially after his recent *The Knockout* fame. Meanwhile, centenarian actress Lisa Lu, honored at the same festival, reminds audiences of cinema’s enduring memory—from her restored classic *The Arch* to the present.

Bridging Generations Through Storytelling

The juxtaposition of Zhang Songwen’s gritty crime drama with Lisa Lu’s elegant reunion shows the festival’s range. Lu, at 103, received a lifetime achievement award, while *The Arch*, her 1970 film, was digitally restored. Her presence counters the violence of *Secret in the Box*, suggesting that cinema is a spectrum of human experience—from brutal to sublime. Yet both films share a core: they refuse to let the past be forgotten. Whether it’s a 1974 cardboard box murder or a 1970s romance, these stories survive, challenging new generations to feel, rage, and remember.

*Secret in the Box* isn’t just entertainment; it’s a cultural artifact. Zhang Songwen’s raw performance, set against the backdrop of a festival daring to tackle taboo, ensures this infamous Hong Kong case will not be laid to rest quietly.
