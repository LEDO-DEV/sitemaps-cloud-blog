Sources: U-M's May finalizing deal to coach Mavs

United States · 2026-06-22

Dusty May's Meteoric Rise: From FAU Final Four to Dallas Mavericks Head Coach

The basketball world is buzzing with news that University of Michigan head coach Dusty May is reportedly finalizing a deal to coach the Dallas Mavericks. In what feels like a Hollywood script, May's trajectory has been nothing short of extraordinary—going from leading Florida Atlantic University to a stunning Final Four appearance to potentially coaching superstars Luka Dončić and Kyrie Irving in the NBA, all within just three years. No college stopover, straight to the big leagues.

A Coaching Career Built on Rapid Success

Dusty May's name first exploded onto the national scene in 2023 when he guided FAU to the Final Four as a No. 9 seed. That Cinderella run wasn't a fluke—he followed it up with another 25-win season, proving his system works. When Michigan came calling in 2024, May accepted, but his tenure in Ann Arbor appears to be brief. Sources now indicate he's finalizing a deal to replace Jason Kidd in Dallas, a move that would reunite him with former Michigan players and give him immediate control of a roster featuring two future Hall of Famers in Dončić and Irving.

The jump from college to the NBA is rare, but May's analytical mind and player-development track record make him an intriguing fit. The Mavericks, coming off a disappointing playoff exit, need a fresh voice to maximize their star duo. May's ability to design creative offenses and build team culture could be exactly what Dallas needs.

What This Means for the Mavericks and NBA Landscape

If finalized, this deal represents a seismic shift. May would be tasked with managing superstar egos while implementing a modern system. For the Mavericks, it signals a move toward innovation over experience. For May, it's the ultimate gamble—but his recent track record suggests he thrives under pressure. The NBA coaching carousel has never seen a rise this fast, but Dusty May has already proven he belongs on the biggest stages.

The next few days will be crucial as contract details are ironed out. One thing is certain: Dusty May has become the hottest name in basketball, and the Dallas Mavericks are betting their future on a coach who went from FAU to the Finals-bound conversation in record time.
