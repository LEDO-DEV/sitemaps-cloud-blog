Sources: Grizzlies trading star Morant to Blazers

United States · 2026-06-30

The NBA world woke up to a seismic shift on Tuesday morning, as reports confirmed that the Memphis Grizzlies are trading superstar guard Ja Morant to the Portland Trail Blazers. In a deal that reshapes the Western Conference, Memphis will receive forward Jerami Grant and wing Kris Murray in return. The announcement, first reported by league sources, brings a stunning end to Morant’s tumultuous tenure in Memphis, a journey that peaked with jaw-dropping highlights and a 56-win season in 2022, only to spiral into a vortex of injuries and off-court controversies.

The Rise and Sudden Fall of Memphis’ Core

Ja Morant’s trajectory was nothing short of phenomenal. After winning Most Improved Player (MIP) in 2022 and leading the Grizzlies to 56 regular-season wins, he seemed destined for superstardom. Alongside Dillon Brooks, Jaren Jackson Jr., and Desmond Bane, the team formed a fierce, young core that promised sustained contention. Yet, within three years, that core four is entirely dismantled. Brooks left in free agency, Jackson Jr. remains but now faces an uncertain rebuild, and Bane is the only constant—albeit without his explosive point guard. The real reason behind this collapse? Availability. Since 2023, Morant has played just 79 games. Injuries, a 33-game suspension for brandishing a gun in a nightclub, and a second incident involving a weapon sidelined him repeatedly, turning a franchise cornerstone into a liability.

What This Means for Portland’s Point Guard Logjam

For the Trail Blazers, this trade is a high-risk, high-reward gamble. Portland now boasts a staggering collection of point guards: Damian Lillard, Jrue Holiday, Scoot Henderson, and Ja Morant. While that depth seems absurd on paper, it signals a potential shift in strategy. Lillard’s future in Portland has been a constant topic of speculation, and acquiring Morant could be a precursor to a blockbuster move involving Lillard, freeing up minutes for Henderson and Morant to build chemistry. However, cramming four starting-caliber point guards into one rotation is a luxury few teams can manage—and a headache for head coach Chauncey Billups. Meanwhile, Memphis gets a proven scorer in Grant, who averaged 21.0 points last season, and a young asset in Kris Murray, but loses the star power that once electrified the league.

In the end, this trade underscores how quickly fortunes can change in the NBA. The Grizzlies are resetting around Jackson Jr. and Bane, hoping for a more stable future. The Blazers, meanwhile, are banking on Morant’s talent outweighing his baggage. Whether this gamble pays off or backfires remains to be seen, but one thing is certain: the Western Conference just got a lot more unpredictable.
