Video of 10 LAPD Vehicles Chasing 2 Kids on a Mini Bike Sparks Debate: “Your Tax Dollars at Work”

United States · 2026-06-23

A video circulating online has ignited a heated debate about police priorities, showing a staggering formation of ten LAPD vehicles chasing two kids on a mini bike through city streets. The clip, which quickly went viral, shows the small dirt bike weaving through traffic while a parade of patrol cars, SUVs, and motorcycles follows close behind. The caption from one viewer sums up the public sentiment: "Nine cop cars chasing two kids on a mini bike is the most LA thing I've ever seen." The spectacle has many asking whether this is a wise use of resources, especially in a city where police response times for serious crimes are often criticized.

The Disconnect Between Budget and Response

The viral mini bike chase video has struck a nerve because it highlights a perceived disconnect between official claims of a police shortage and the apparent willingness to deploy a massive fleet for a low-stakes pursuit. Critics point out that while the LAPD frequently cites budget constraints and understaffing, the decision to greenlight ten vehicles for two juveniles on a non-street-legal vehicle seems contradictory. As one commenter put it, "You're telling me there's a police shortage and budget issues, but somehow 10 vehicles get greenlit for this? Meanwhile people can't get a response for actual burglaries." The math, as they say, isn't adding up for many taxpayers who feel their dollars are being spent on high-visibility chases rather than community safety.

Safety Risks and Policy Questions

Beyond the budget debate, the mini bike chase also raises serious questions about public safety. High-speed pursuits, even of small vehicles, pose risks to bystanders, officers, and the riders themselves. In dense urban areas like Los Angeles, a chase involving multiple police cars can quickly escalate into a dangerous situation. The kids on the mini bike, likely facing relatively minor citations for riding without a license or registration, ended up in a nerve-wracking encounter with law enforcement. This incident forces a conversation about whether alternative responses—like tracking the bike’s owner via license plate or following at a safe distance—might be more prudent than deploying a fleet of patrol units.

Ultimately, this video serves as a powerful visual metaphor for broader questions about policing priorities. While the LAPD will likely defend the chase as necessary for enforcing traffic laws and preventing future reckless behavior, the public sees a glaring mismatch between the scale of the response and the nature of the offense. As one observer noted, the real crime here is the math ain't mathing. For now, the image of ten police vehicles chasing two kids on a mini bike will remain a potent symbol for critics who feel that tax dollars aren't always spent where they matter most.
