Ja Morant going from a 56-win season and Rookie of the Year

United States · 2026-07-01

The NBA landscape is littered with "what ifs," but few stories pivot from dizzying heights to a precipitous fall quite like Ja Morant’s recent trajectory. Just three years ago, Morant was the undisputed future of the league, leading the Memphis Grizzlies to a 56-win season and taking home the Rookie of the Year award. His explosive athleticism and fearless play made him a superstar in the making. Now, the reality is jarring: sources confirm the Grizzlies are trading the star guard to the Portland Trail Blazers for Jerami Grant and Kris Murray. On paper, this swap feels like a shocking devaluation, prompting a natural question: where does this rank among the fastest star-player flameouts in NBA history?

The Context of a Rapid Descent

When discussing Ja Morant and his fall from grace, it is essential to look beyond the trade itself. The transition from being the face of a 56-win franchise to a player moved for a role player and a developing prospect is almost unprecedented in such a short timeframe. For comparison, consider other flameouts: players like Shawn Kemp or Derrick Rose saw declines tied to injury or personal issues, but their trade value often remained higher than what Memphis is reportedly accepting now. Morant’s situation is unique because it combines on-court brilliance with off-court controversies that have eroded his value faster than a typical athletic decline. The Grizzlies, once built around him, are now pivoting to a new core, which signals a complete philosophical shift.

Where This Ranks in All-Time Flameouts

In the pantheon of fast collapses, Morant’s case is entering rare air. We often see stars fade due to age or injury, but a player going from a 56-win season and Rookie of the Year to being traded for Jerami Grant and Kris Murray in just three years is a startlingly quick de-escalation. While LeBron James continues to defy time at age 41, averaging 27 points a game, Morant’s career arc has bent in the opposite direction. Even recent examples like Jalen Duren, who averaged 19.5 and 10.5 this season to make the All-Star team, show how quickly young talent can ascend – making Morant’s drop seem even more severe. Historically, you might compare it to a player like Gilbert Arenas, whose career unraveled due to a single incident, but Morant’s multiple suspensions have created a pattern that scared teams away.

The Unpredictable Future

While the trade to Portland offers a fresh start, the cost of this move will be a lasting footnote. For the Blazers, acquiring a player with Morant’s talent is a gamble worth taking, but the trade package of Grant and Murray suggests the league is valuing him as a high-risk asset rather than a franchise savior. The question remains whether he can rebuild his reputation in a new city or if this marks the beginning of a longer slide. For now, Ja Morant going from a 56-win season and Rookie of the Year to traded for Jerami Grant and Kris Murray in three years stands as one of the most dramatic and cautionary tales in modern NBA history – a reminder that talent alone cannot shelter a star from the consequences of their actions.
