Clark rides roller coaster to 2nd U.S. Open crown

United States · 2026-06-21

Wyndham Clark is riding the ultimate roller coaster at this year’s U.S. Open, and it’s not the kind you find at an amusement park. After a blistering start at Shinnecock Hills—a first-round 64 followed by a second-round 69—Clark seized the outright lead at 7-under. But the path to a second U.S. Open crown has been anything but smooth, haunted by a very different memory from just one year ago.

The Ghosts of Oakmont

Clark’s 2025 U.S. Open campaign at Oakmont is infamous for all the wrong reasons. After smashing a locker in frustration and missing the cut, he spent the last year answering for that explosive moment. “I’ve gotten a lot of grief since last year, rightfully so. I’m hoping I can win back the fans,” Clark admitted. That raw emotion—the same fire that broke a locker—is now the only thing keeping him in front at Shinnecock. Fans watching the drama unfold see a player who is grinding through wayward drives on 14, 15, and 16, somehow emerging 1-over instead of 3-over, then pouring in a 33-footer at 18 to stay atop the leaderboard.

Grinding Through the Chaos

Clark’s roller coaster ride is a masterclass in resilience. His game has been erratic, but his mental toughness is rewriting the narrative. Instead of letting the pressure sink his second U.S. Open bid, he’s channeling that past anger into focus. Every wayward drive and clutch putt feels like a metaphor for his journey—wild, unpredictable, yet somehow heading toward a second U.S. Open crown. If he can keep the ride on track, he might just earn back the fans he lost at Oakmont.

Conclusion

Wyndham Clark’s path to the U.S. Open title is a testament to growth under fire. Whether he holds on to win or stumbles again, this roller coaster has already given golf fans a story worth following. The question now: Can Clark’s fire forge a legacy, or will the ghosts of Oakmont strike again?
