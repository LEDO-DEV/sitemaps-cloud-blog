Clark rides roller coaster to 2nd U.S. Open crown

United States · 2026-06-22

A Roller Coaster Ride to Redemption

The U.S. Open is known for punishing the faint of heart, but Wyndham Clark is proving he thrives in chaos. After a gritty 69 on Friday that kept him in contention, the 30-year-old rolled in a 33-foot bomb on 18 to lock in the lead at Shinnecock Hills. Now, as he flirts with a second U.S. Open crown in three years, Clark is asking for forgiveness over the Oakmont locker incident—a moment that nearly derailed his career. But on Long Island, he’s rewriting the narrative, turning a potential scandal into a story of resilience.

The Grit Behind the Glow

Clark’s path to the top of the leaderboard hasn’t been smooth. The Oakmont locker incident, where he reportedly made inappropriate comments about a fellow player’s equipment, earned him a reprimand from the Tour. Yet, instead of crumbling, he channeled that embarrassment into focus. His Friday 69 was a masterclass in damage control—saving pars from impossible lies and grinding through the wind. Then came the 33-foot bomb on 18, a putt that seemed to erase all doubts. “I’ve been working on keeping my emotions in check,” Clark said post-round. “That putt was for everyone who thought I couldn’t handle the pressure.” The crowd at Shinnecock, initially skeptical, roared in approval.

Context Matters: From Scandal to Stardom

The U.S. Open has a history of redemption arcs, but Clark’s story is uniquely modern. After Oakmont, he faced criticism for being “too cocky” and “undisciplined.” Critics wondered if he had the mental fortitude for major championship golf. Yet here he is, sitting alone atop the leaderboard, with a chance to win a second U.S. Open crown in three years. His game has matured—his driving accuracy has improved, and his short game is razor sharp. But it’s the mental shift that stands out. Clark is no longer just a talented slugger; he’s a strategist, carefully plotting his way around a brutal Shinnecock layout that has swallowed bigger names.

Wyndham Clark’s roller coaster ride at Shinnecock is far from over. With a second U.S. Open crown in his sights, he’s turned a potential distraction into a driving force. If he can keep his nerve—and his putter hot—he might just pull off the most improbable win since… well, since the Oakmont locker incident. The golf world is watching. So is his own redemption.
