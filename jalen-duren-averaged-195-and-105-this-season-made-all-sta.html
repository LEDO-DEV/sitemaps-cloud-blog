Jalen Duren averaged 19.5 and 10.5 this season, made All-Sta

United States · 2026-06-30

Jalen Duren’s rise has been one of the more intriguing storylines of the season. The 22-year-old center averaged an impressive 19.5 points and 10.5 rebounds during the regular season, earning his first All-Star selection and even receiving All-NBA votes. That level of production at such a young age usually signals a franchise cornerstone. However, the playoffs painted a different picture. Duren’s numbers dropped to 10.2 points and 8.5 rebounds per game, and he was eventually benched in favor of Paul Reed. This sharp contrast between regular-season dominance and postseason struggles has created a fascinating dilemma for the Detroit Pistons and any potential trade partners.

The Contract Question and Trade Rumors

According to recent reports, Jalen Duren is seeking a contract worth over $40 million per year. That is a massive financial commitment for a player whose playoff performance raised questions about his consistency. The proposed trade scenario has an interesting twist: Sacramento Kings’ Domantas Sabonis could be the chip coming back the other way. Sabonis, a proven All-Star and one of the league’s best passing big men, would immediately change the dynamic for any team. For the Pistons, swapping a young, rising center for an established veteran might make sense if they want to win now. But for Sacramento, the question is whether they would take a flier on Duren’s upside or stick with Sabonis’ reliability.

What Makes Sense for Sacramento

The Kings have a decision to make. Duren offers youth, athleticism, and a higher ceiling as a rim protector and rebounder. Sabonis provides elite playmaking and scoring from the post but can be a defensive liability. If Sacramento believes Jalen Duren can recapture his regular-season form and develop into a more consistent playoff performer, this trade could be a smart long-term move. However, the risk is real. Duren’s postseason benching shows he still has a lot to prove. For a team like the Kings, who are trying to build momentum, trading a known commodity for an unknown potential could backfire.

In the end, this proposed swap highlights the volatility of the NBA trade market. A 22-year-old with All-Star credentials and a desire for a max contract might be worth the gamble, but only if his playoff struggles are an outlier. Sacramento will have to weigh Duren’s ceiling against Sabonis’ proven floor. Whether the Kings make the move or not, this scenario underscores how quickly a player’s value can swing—from All-Star to benchwarmer, and back again.
