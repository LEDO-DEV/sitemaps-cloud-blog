CD Projekt Red co-CEO admits it "indefinitely" "lost the faith" of some fans after Cyberpunk 2077

United States · 2026-06-21

When a launch goes this wrong, even millions of sales can't fix everything. CD Projekt Red co-CEO has admitted that the studio "indefinitely" lost the faith of some fans after the disastrous launch of *Cyberpunk 2077*, and no amount of post-launch fixes or blockbuster expansions can fully reverse that damage. The admission came as a sobering moment for a studio once hailed as the darling of the gaming world, now grappling with the long-term consequences of a release that became a cautionary tale.

The Rise, Fall, and Slow Climb Back

*Cyberpunk 2077* launched in December 2020 to astronomical hype, only to crash under the weight of bugs, performance issues, and missing features — especially on last-gen consoles. The backlash was swift and brutal. Refunds were issued, Sony pulled the game from the PlayStation Store, and CD Projekt Red's reputation, built on the back of *The Witcher 3*, took a nosedive. Since then, the studio has poured resources into patches, free DLC, and the critically acclaimed expansion *Phantom Liberty*, which earned rave reviews. Sales have climbed past 35 million copies, proving that many players came back — but not everyone.

Why "Indefinitely" Matters

The co-CEO's choice of the word "indefinitely" is telling. It acknowledges that for a segment of the fanbase, trust is not something that can be rebuilt with updates or expansions. Even a successful redemption arc has its limits. When you promise the moon and deliver a broken skyline, some relationships are simply severed beyond repair. This is a crucial lesson for any game studio: launch matters more than patches. The damage done to the CD Projekt Red brand among those burned by the *Cyberpunk 2077* launch is permanent, and the company now faces an uphill battle.

The Witcher 4 as a Faith Restoration Project

That’s where *The Witcher 4* comes in. CD Projekt Red is now banking heavily on the next installment in the series that originally defined their excellence. The hope is that a return to familiar, beloved territory — combined with a flawless launch — will win back the faith they burned. The stakes are incredibly high. If *The Witcher 4* launches with the same polish and depth that made *The Witcher 3* a masterpiece, it could go a long way toward healing the wound. But if it stumbles, the "lost" fans may never return.

In the end, the gaming industry is ruthless, and trust is hard to earn but easy to lose. CD Projekt Red's co-CEO understands that some fans are gone for good — and the only path forward is to make a game that makes the rest forget the pain.
