The AJ Dybantsa Pick: Why the Jazz Might Have Just Landed a Future Superstar

When the Utah Jazz selected AJ Dybantsa with the No. 1 pick in the 2025 NBA Draft, few were surprised. The 6'9" wing out of high school basketball powerhouse programs had been projected as the top prospect for months. But the chatter around this selection is louder than usual, with some calling him "overrated" even before he laces up his NBA sneakers. The reality is far more nuanced — and far more exciting.

AJ Dybantsa’s prep career was nothing short of historic. Averaging 37 points per game in his senior season, he displayed a scoring arsenal that included deep three-pointers, acrobatic finishes, and a mid-range game that reminded scouts of a young Kevin Durant. Yet, the whispers of "overrated" persist, often tied to concerns about his competition level or his perceived tendency to "chuck" shots. What gets lost is his elite length, fluid athleticism, and a basketball IQ that allowed him to dominate without relying solely on brute force.

The real story, though, is what this means for the Utah Jazz. They didn't just take the consensus No. 1 — they bet their entire rebuild on a player whose comparison is a taller, more explosive version of prime Paul George. Dybantsa’s game shares the same silky handles, defensive versatility, and effortless scoring, but with fewer question marks off the court. If he develops a reliable three-point shot and stays healthy, the Jazz may have secured the steal of the decade. In two years, those calling him "mid" will likely be eating their words, as his combination of size and skill is a blueprint for modern NBA stardom.

The take nobody says out loud is that Dybantsa’s ceiling is a top-10 player in the league, and Utah's patience in holding onto their picks finally pays off. For now, the debate rages on, but the court will have the final word. If he stays healthy, the Jazz didn't just draft a prospect — they drafted a franchise cornerstone.
