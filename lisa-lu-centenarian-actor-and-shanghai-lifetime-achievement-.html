Lisa Lu, Centenarian Actor and Shanghai Lifetime Achievement Honoree, Reunites With Fans at Screening of Restored ‘The Arch’

United States · 2026-06-21

Lisa Lu, the centenarian actor and recent Shanghai Lifetime Achievement honoree, continues to defy age and inspire audiences. At 98, she received her star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame, a milestone few actors ever achieve. Now, at 100 years old, Lu reunited with fans at a special screening of the restored version of her 1970 film *The Arch*, the very movie that earned her the Best Actress award. The event celebrated both her enduring legacy and the preservation of classic cinema, drawing attention to her remarkable career that spans decades and continents.

A Career That Crossed Generations and Cultures

Lu’s career is a testament to her talent and resilience. She co-starred with Hollywood legend James Stewart in the 1960 film *The Mountain Road*, marking her as one of the few Asian actors to work in mainstream American cinema during that era. Decades later, she charmed a new generation when she played the grandmother in *Crazy Rich Asians* at age 91, proving that her appeal transcends time. The screening of *The Arch*, a groundbreaking film directed by Cecilia Kang, showcased a story of female desire and societal repression, with Lu’s performance still resonating powerfully today. The restoration of the film underscores its cultural significance and Lu’s role in advancing Asian representation in cinema.

The Shanghai Honor and Fan Reunion

The Shanghai International Film Festival recently awarded Lu a Lifetime Achievement Award, recognizing her contributions to both Chinese and global cinema. At the screening, fans gathered to celebrate not just the film but the woman behind it. Lu’s presence was a reminder of her longevity and dedication to her craft. Even at 100, she engaged warmly with the audience, sharing anecdotes from a career that bridged classic Hollywood and modern Asian cinema. The event also highlighted the importance of film restoration, ensuring that works like *The Arch* remain accessible for future generations to appreciate Lu’s artistry.

Lu’s reunion with fans reaffirms her place as a beloved icon. Her journey from a young actress in Shanghai to a centenarian honoree in Hollywood is a story of perseverance and passion. As she continues to inspire, her legacy proves that age is no barrier to artistic impact.
