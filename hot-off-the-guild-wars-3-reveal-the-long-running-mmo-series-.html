Hot off the Guild Wars 3 reveal, the long-running MMO series is getting its first-ever CCG in Mistbound

United States · 2026-06-21

The gaming world was rocked this week by the reveal of *Guild Wars 3*, the next major chapter in ArenaNet’s beloved MMORPG legacy. After a 21-year PC tradition, the developer broke a significant barrier by announcing that the console version of *Guild Wars 3* will be a PlayStation 5 exclusive, skipping Xbox entirely. This bold move for Sony’s ecosystem has left many fans wondering about the future of the franchise, especially with the simultaneous reveal of *Mistbound*, the series’ first-ever collectible card game (CCG). While the MMO is generating massive hype, the exclusivity deal raises questions about whether *Mistbound* will be cross-platform or remain tied to the same console restrictions.

Guild Wars 3 Breaks a 21-Year PC Tradition

For over two decades, the *Guild Wars* series has been a staple of PC gaming, with its deep lore, player-driven economy, and action-oriented combat. The announcement of *Guild Wars 3* marks a paradigm shift, as ArenaNet has never released a mainline title on consoles. By making the PlayStation 5 the exclusive home for the console version, the studio is betting on Sony’s ecosystem to bring new players into the fold. This decision is a strategic one, aiming to tap into a mature install base eager for high-fidelity MMOs. However, it also alienates Xbox players who have been loyal to the franchise on PC, creating a fractured community before the game even launches.

Mistbound Introduces a New CCG Frontier

Alongside the MMO, the *Guild Wars 3* reveal included *Mistbound*, a CCG that expands the Tyrian universe into card-based strategy. This marks a first for the series, which has historically focused on cooperative and competitive PvE and PvP gameplay. The CCG is expected to leverage the rich lore of the Mists, offering players a new way to engage with iconic characters and skills. Currently, there is no official word on whether *Mistbound* will be cross-platform, but given the PlayStation 5 exclusivity for *Guild Wars 3*, many speculate that the card game may follow suit. This would be a missed opportunity for cross-play synergy, especially as CCGs thrive on large player bases.

The Future of a Connected Universe

The choice to go exclusive with Sony is a gamble that could pay off handsomely if *Guild Wars 3* delivers on its promise of immersive storytelling and expansive worlds. Yet, the lack of clarity around *Mistbound*’s platform availability leaves a lingering question: Are these two games meant to be played together, or are they separate experiences? For now, PC and PlayStation 5 users can look forward to diving back into Tyria, while Xbox fans are left waiting—or hoping that *Mistbound* offers a more inclusive path forward for the franchise.
